3 Work space

Upon choosing the configuration file, the GUI shown in Figure 6 is displayed. An overview of its main elements is presented in this section. More detailed descriptions are given in later sections.

Most graphical elements have tooltips, i.e., short explanations that are displayed if the mouse is placed over the element.

3.1 Title bar

The Title Bar displays two items, Model and Sources. These items are always visible and are used to handle probability models and marked record pair sources. Initially, no model or source is selected. Many menu items are disabled until a model and at least one source are selected.

3.2 Menu and tool bar

The menu and toolbars are used for issuing commands. The toolbar also contains the current settings of the differ and match thresholds. For regression-based machine learning techniques, these thresholds determine the probabilistic decision.

Values between 0 and 101 are allowed. Values greater than 100 are allowed to have a value that is guaranteed to be higher than any computed probability. The differ threshold cannot be higher than the match threshold. Setting the two thresholds to the same value results in zero pairs with a probabilistic decision of hold. Rules can still create hold decisions and, thereby, human review pairs.

3.3 Tabbed panes

There are three tabbed panes corresponding to the main activities that one performs in the course of building a probability model: Training, Testing, and Reviewing. One moves from pane to pane by clicking on the appropriate tab. When visible, a tabbed pane may cause additional, context-dependent, menu items to be displayed on the menu bar.

3.4 Message panel

The purpose of the message panel (at the bottom of the screen) is to display status messages to the user. These can be information, warning, or error messages. This panel is always visible, but its height can be adjusted by dragging its upper border.

3.5 Pairs list

The pairs list, at the right of the screen, shows the indices (numbers starting from 0) of the pairs selected by the active filter. Clicking on an index switches to the review panel and shows the specified pair. The pair list can be hidden through the View menu.
