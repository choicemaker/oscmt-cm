Chapter III. ChoiceMaker schemas

1 Introduction

Because of the great variety of ways that clients store data and the even greater variety of kinds of data that they store, every deployment of ChoiceMaker is somewhat different. One of ChoiceMaker's most outstanding characteristics is that this variety is accommodated without requiring the hand editing of any ChoiceMaker source code. This is achieved through its modular design and two key components - the ChoiceMaker schema and the clue set. The former defines the type and structure of the `things' to be matched as well as the data exchange with the client applications and database, while the latter defines how comparisons are to be made. We describe ChoiceMaker schemas in this chapter and ClueMaker in the next.
