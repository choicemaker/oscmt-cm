9 Testing

Once a probability model and marked record pair source have been selected, the testing panel can be used to evaluate the model and display various performance statistics as shown in Figure 16. The model is evaluated by choosing Model->Evaluate, Model->Train, or Model->Train and Test.In the latter case, all statistics are based on the newly trained model.

To produce statistically sound accuracy measures in testing, use separate sources for training and testing. Training and testing on the same source is `cheating.' All statistics are for the specified test set and may not carry over to other test sets with different characteristics.

For regression-based machine learning techniques, the current differ and match thresholds (in the toolbar) play a role in the computation of most of the statistics in the upper portion of the testing panel. They do not apply to any of the data plots or the calculator in the lower part.

9.1 Values based on current thresholds

The upper part of the testing panel displays several statistical values that, for regression-based machine learning techniques, are based on the current values of the differ and match thresholds.

9.2 Confusion matrix

The confusion matrix is displayed in the upper part of the testing panel. This table shows how ChoiceMaker assigned match/differ/hold to the marked record pairs given the specified match/differ thresholds and how often they correspond to the human decisions (it charts ChoiceMaker's "confusion" of the correct answer with the incorrect answers). For example, the first row of the table (Figure 16) shows that of all the record-pairs human-marked differ, ChoiceMaker assigned 2488 of them as differ, 30 of them as match, and 67 of them as hold. Note that in this example the record pair source contained no records human-marked hold. All of the numbers in red can be considered as ChoiceMaker errors. Ideally we would like the values in red to all be zero.

Clicking on any cell of the confusion matrix sets the record pair filter, displayed on the right side of the screen. For example, the record pair indices shown in Figure 16 are the result of clicking on the highlighted cell of the confusion matrix.

Correlation

The statistical correlation between the ChoiceMaker probabilities and the human marked probabilities given the following mapping:

human-marked match ® human marked probability = 1.0

human-marked hold ® human marked probability = 0.5

human-marked differ ® human marked probability = 0.0

Note that the correlation does not depend on the differ and match thresholds and that it does not take into account rules.

9.4 Plots and overview data

The lower part of the testing panel displays one of several different statistical plots or a `calculator'. All data displayed here is independent of the current values of the thresholds.

All plots can be zoomed in or out by clicking and dragging the mouse from over the desired area. Dragging the mouse from upper left to lower right zooms into the area of the graph selected, while dragging from upper right to lower left zooms out to the default setting. Zooming is also available via the context menu. All plots also have tool tips that show exact values in textual form.
